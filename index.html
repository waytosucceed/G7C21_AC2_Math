QUADRILATERALS

The term quadrilateral is derived from the Latin terms 'Quadra' meaning four and 'Latus' meaning 'sides'. A quadrilateral's four sides do not have to be the same length. As a result, we may classify quadrilaterals according to their sides and angles.

What is a Quadrilateral?

A quadrilateral is a plane shape with four sides (edges) and four corners (vertices). The quadrilateral's angles can be found at its four vertices or corners. If ABCD is a quadrilateral then:

  • Angles at the vertices = ∠A, ∠B, ∠C and ∠D
  • Sides of a quadrilateral = AB, BC, CD and DA.

Types of Quadrilaterals

The different types of quadrilaterals are determined by the angles and lengths of their sides.

As the word 'quad' signifies four, all of these quadrilaterals have four sides, and the sum of their angles is 360 degrees.

Quadrilaterals are classified into the following types:

Rectangle

It is a quadrilateral in which the opposite sides are equal and parallel to each other and all the angles of a rectangle are equal to 90°. A rectangle shape is characterised by two dimensions, its length and width. The longer side of the rectangle is known as the length and the shorter side is known as the width. Property: The diagonals of a rectangle are of equal length.



Square: A square is a rectangular shape with equal sides. This indicates that a square has all of the features of a rectangle, with the added constraint that all sides be the same length. The square, like the rectangle, has diagonals of the same length. Property: A square's diagonals are perpendicular bisectors to one another.

Rhombus: A rhombus is a quadrilateral with sides that have the same length. A rhombus is also a parallelogram since its opposite sides are equal in length. So a rhombus combines the features of a parallelogram and a kite. Property: The diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular bisectors of one another.

Parallelogram
A parallelogram is a quadrilateral. As the name implies, it has something to do with parallel lines. A parallelogram consists of four sides and four angles. A rectangle, a rhombus, and a square are special parallelograms because they not only show the properties of a parallelogram but also have unique properties of their own. Properties of parallelogram:

  1. A parallelogram's opposite sides are equal in length.
  2. A parallelogram's opposite angles have equal measure.
  3. A parallelogram's diagonals are typically not of equal length.
  4. A parallelogram's diagonals bisect each other at the intersection point.


Trapezium
It is a quadrilateral with a pair of parallel sides. Kite
Kite is a special type of quadrilateral. The sides with the same markings in each figure are equal. For example AB = AD and BC = CD. There are exactly two distinct consecutive pairs of sides of equal length.
